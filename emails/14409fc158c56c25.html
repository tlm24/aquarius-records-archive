Beloved Customers and Friends:

Hey people! This week's in-between list is a little different than

normal. For one thing, we're doing it on a Thursday (more on the

reason for that in a second), and also, rather than listing a bunch of

stuff we've previously reviewed, like we usually do, instead it's a

list of LISTS, your lists, of the Best Of 2013! Yeah, remember those

2013 Top Ten lists you made (or didn't make)? We asked you to send 'em

in, a bunch of you did, and now we're presenting all the lists, big

and small that all you wonderful music-obsessive aQ customers

submitted! You'll find 'em all below, and also posted here:

http://www.aquariusrecords.org/cat/4435.html.

Every year we're so excited to see your lists. Sometimes, it's nice

'cause we see a lot of things we can sometimes assume you heard about

from our reviews. And a lot of the time, it's fascinating 'cause we

hear about so much more cool stuff from you.

So we recommended doing what we do, and going carefully through these

lists and looking stuff up and checking it out. Your fellow music

lovers most likely have some good tips, some finds you might not have

already made.

A bunch of you sent in nice words about aQ that we've mostly omitted

here (but thanks!). Several list makers also provided commentary on

each of their picks, which is great - we've included it when it was in

the sentence-or-two range, but some others were just too long to

include, sorry!

Many of our regular customers submitted lists - but we do always

notice that some regulars don't. C'mon, we KNOW you make year end best

of lists - who doesn't?? So next year, we hope to see yours. (Yes, we

recommend you start working on your 2014 lists NOW!)

Also, although we DO have plenty of female customers, we couldn't help

but notice that it was mostly - or maybe entirely - guys who submitted

lists this year. Perhaps not a shocker. But still... let's hear from

the ladies next time too, ok?

Anyway, everybody who did, thanks for sharing!

All right - another bit of business - we have randomly selected the

usual two lucky listmakers to win $25 aQ gift certificates. This

year's winners: Louis Libert of Oakland, and Greg Groff from right

here in SF!! Congrats!!

Oh, and if you want to see more lists, you can also visit our page for

the aQ staff's favorites of 2013 here:

http://www.aquariusrecords.org/stafffaves2013.html

And, of course, this being an in-betweener, even though there are no

reviews, you can still order stuff off of the "instock, not yet

reviewed" section of recent arrivals that you'll find a the bottom of

this list. Furthermore, don't forget that last week's exciting list

#443 is still eager for your attention here:

http://www.aquariusrecords.org/cat/newest.html

We've still got copies of all 3 Records Of The Week, among other

things, so please check it out if you were too busy last weekend with,

like, the Superbowl or something.

As for this weekend - if you live in Southern California, you should

definitely try to make it to see Andee's band A MINOR FOREST's reunion

shows in LA and San Diego!! (That's why we're doing this today, 'cause

Andee won't be here tomorrow, he'll be in San Diego). Friday is in San

Diego at the Casbah, and Saturday is in L.A. at the Satellite

(formerly Spaceland). Both shows are with Rob Crow's (from Pinback /

Heavy Vegetable) new band, and the San Diego show will also feature a

rare performance by elusive aQ fave LESSER!! More info about both

shows can be found HERE:

SD: https://www.facebook.com/events/207811129421657/

LA: https://www.facebook.com/events/746776292017103/

Ok, on with the 2013 aQ customer faves!!

---------------------------------------------------------------

And as always, thanks for reading the list, passing it on to all your

friends who love weird music, shopping at our store, turning -us- on

to all sort of great stuff, and helping us spread the word and get all

this great music to the people who love it. YOU!! And as always,

please realize that we work really hard on the list, so if you find

out about stuff through us, please try to buy your records from us.

That way we can keep on doing what we do, and we'll always be here

with our ears to the ground, and with cds full of metalcore pitbulls,

death metal parrots, gamelan playing elephants, recordings of glaciers

cracking, ice melting, zamboni's, life support systems, drag races,

audience applause, and of course self flagellating Norwegian dwarves,

moaning telephone wires, recorded exorcisms, acapella straight edge

metalcore, high school battles of the bands, movie theater organ

music, Christian psychedelic folk, Bhangra Black Sabbath as well as

all the metal, indie rock, electronica, punk rock, reggae, dub,

sixties psych, krautrock, classic rock, country and anything else your

heart may desire. So thanks. A bunch!

FERRARA BRAIN PAN

The Top ELEVEN Albums Of 2013, According To Ferrara Brain Pan

1. GOLDFRAPP: Tales Of Us - lush, bittersweet, romantic orchestral pop

in the manner of Scott Walker's first four albums

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wCzoTQqEoFg

2. MUSICCARGO: Harmonie - the most ingenious, infectious Motorik

krautrock since NEU! and Kraftwerk laid the foundations back in the 70s

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MSMvR4O3DFQ

3. ANNA VON HAUSSWOLFF: Ceremony - think vintage Dead Can Dance with a

cathedral pipe organ and Kate Bush on vocals (originally came out in

Sweden in 2012 but only released in the US this past July)

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uABaTw73PFU

4. ZINOVIA: The Gift Of Affliction - poignantly elegiac and cinematic

orchestral electronica, haunting and melancholic

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gAOCJbUrMeU

5. DAUGHTER: If You Leave - well crafted ballads of heartbreak and

defeat in a 4AD ethereal pop vein (the true sound of a Suicide Girl)

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zGnAsQobwrw

6. WREKMEISTER HARMONIES: You've Always Meant So Much To Me - epic

instrumental composition, equal parts minimalist dirgo, chamber

orchestra and metallic onslaught

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eR7QqPMDECc

7. FUNCTION: Incubation - brilliantly dark and compelling techno,

reminded me of Clock DVA but a thousand times better

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4m6sMUjB1YQ

8. WARDRUNA: Runaljod: Yggdrasil - Nordic primal atavistic resurgence

folk-metal, carrying the torch from Dead Can Dance

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5Xt181Nf7Po

9. THE HAXAN CLOAK: Excavation - dark and delirious nightmare techno

soundscapes, deep and disturbing, more like an 'evisceration' than an

'excavation'

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3Cq3nwR1www

10. ROBERT HAIGH: Darkling Streams - pensive, minor key piano

miniatures, rather Satiesque, with minimal electronics

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8o2JiGC-cgI

11. ZOLA JESUS: Versions - achingly beautiful string quartet

arrangements of her grave gothic anthems

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9HFw3yOuauw

And here is a link to my four-hour radio show playing twenty-seven of

the best releases of the year, with podcast and playlist:

http://radiovalencia.fm/rocknrollstation/2013/12/26/rock-n-roll-station-remembrancer/

ERICK HALLSTROM

Best Albums of 2013:

Deafheaven - Sunbather. Post black metal interspersed with atmospheric

samples and delicate piano interludes. Creatively complex and holds

together as an album exceedingly well.

Oranssi Pazuzu - Valonielu. Post black metal from Finland with a hint

of industrial and/or krautrock mixed in. It s heavy, yet catchy, and

hard to define.

Run The Jewels - Run The Jewels. Amazing hip hop collaboration between

Killer Mike and El-P that is it as urgent and intense as it is

theatrical. Kept me on the edge of my seat from the first beat to the

last.

Subrosa - More Constant Than the Gods. Heavy doom metal from Salt Lake

City. Shared male and female vocals occasionally cause one to forget

how heavy and churning this beautiful album is.

Uncle Acid & the Deadbeats - Mind Control. Psychedelic heavy rock from

the U.K. that nods aggressively to early proto-metal and hard rock.

William Tyler - Impossible Truth. Mesmerizingly beautiful acoustic

guitar instrumentals that tell a story in a way few solo

instrumentalists can.

The Body - Christs, Redeemers. Terrifying is the only way I can

describe the Body. Post-metal, post-noise. Blood curdlingly and

ingeniously artistic at every turn.

Daft Punk - Random Access Memories. Need I describe this album?

Despite the heavy hitters appearing, the track Georgio by Morodor is

what sealed the deal for me.

Iron Reagan - Worse Than Dead. Throwback hardcore from a hotbed of

innovative metal and hardcore, Richmond, VA. This 19 track album is 25

minutes long and includes tracks such as Midlothian Murder Mile and

Eyes Piss Tears. What s not to love?

Monomyth - Monomyth. Some strange combination of semi-electronic

tinged instrumental krautrock and heavy stoner jams, it works

perfectly for this band from the Netherlands.

Runners Up:

Pelican - Forever Becoming. They are one of my favorites, but this

album didn t quite make the top 10. Heavy instrumental post-rock.

Vhol - Vhol. American black metal mixed with technical thrash by a

line up of Bay area heavy hitters.

Clutch - Earth Rocker. Straight up rock and roll from a band that has

been putting out rock albums from outside of DC for as long as I can

remember. They still got it.

Windhand - Soma. No sophomore slump here, this album is epic and

surpasses their first record. So heavy and dark. Another Virginia band

to be proud of.

Sigur Ros - Kveikur. In my opinion, this is the most sparse and jagged

Sigur Ros album to date. It is still delicate and gorgeous. But a

welcome departure from these Icelandic legends.

Albums That I Discovered (or rediscovered) Late by Reading Other Best

of Lists that I wish I would have Listened to More This Year:

Lorde - Pure Heroine

Phosphorescent - Muchacho

Melvins - Tres Cabrones

Deltron 3030 - Event II

Life Coach - Alphawaves

Four Tet - Beautiful Rewind

M.I.A. - Mantangi

Whores - Clean

Earl Sweatshirt - Doris

Best Electronica / Ambient:

Pantha du Prince & the Bell Laboratory - Elements of Light. One of my

favorites. Rocks the bells.

Burial - Rival Dealer EP. This EP is longer than Iron Reagan s LP. And

crammed with excellence.

Recondite - Hinterlands. Sparse like the hinterlands, yet rhythmic.

It elegantly fills the empty space.

The Haxon Cloak - Excavation. Dark, brooding, atmospheric, human.

Best Reissue / Box Set:

Unwound - Kid is Gone. This is the first of a 4 party box set series,

covering the earliest years of Unwound s existence. If you were into

punk, hardcore, or grunge in the 90s, you know what Unwound offers.

The remastering here makes a big difference on some of the early demos

and live recordings.

Best Soundtrack:

Mogwai - Les Revenants. Of course Mogwai would make the soundtrack to

a post-modern French television show about zombies who come back from

the dead and then just hang around and cause logistical problems.

Amazing.
