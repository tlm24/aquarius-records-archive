And for those who might not be so familiar with Direen, here's more

about the man, his bands, his place in underground NZ rock history,

and more, from our pal Dan, aka Clarinette:

Bill Direen is a legend in underground New Zealand music having been

one of the earliest artists to record for New Zealand's Flying Nun

Records (in fact Direen's Bilders' album Beatin' Hearts was the very

first LP recorded by Flying Nun) and one of the earliest active

artists in the nation's late 70's garage punk scene with his band

Vacuum (whose members would go on to form The Victor Dimisich Band and

The Terminals and who were recently anthologized with a pair of 'from

the vaults' 7"s on Siltbreeze Records...Siltbreeze also recently

reissued Direen's early cassette under the name Above Ground on LP).

He has recorded under many names and with many collaborators but

primarily with his band The Bilders (or Builders, depending on the

record), whose recordings feature an ever changing collection of NZ

luminaries including Chris Knox, Alec Bathgate, Alastair Galbraith and

members of The Bats, Axemen, The Terminals, The Clean and The

Renderers). Direen was pivotal in the early 1980's New Zealand DIY

scene self releasing his albums on his own South Indies Records

imprint and more recently releasing his work on the Powertool label.

Direen has most recently seen a series of his classic early 80's EP's

reissued as limited edition 12" EP's on the German label Unwucht. His

music is truly original though traces of The Velvet Underground and

60's garage and psychedelia slip in and out of his sound. Direen is

also a writer, having released numerous novels, collections of poetry

and theater pieces and being named University of Auckland Writer

Fellow of 2010. The Aquarius in-store will be followed in the evening

by a performance at Oakland's White Horse Bar (6551 Telegraph Ave.)

with Dan Melchior, Little Queenie and Musk.

Please join us for this very special performance, should be amazing!
