Hey everybody!!

Just wanted to send out a quick announcement for our next instore,

we've mentioned it on the last couple New Arrivals lists, and various

other places, but we're pretty dang excited, so we figured it deserved

an actual proper email-list invitation. It's happening this coming

Monday, and will feature a live performance by none other than

longtime aQ faves and Wooden Shjips offshoot, psychedelic spacekraut

hypnorock combo Moon Duo. We're so psyched. It should be amazing.

We made their most recent album Mazes, or Record Of The Week a few

weeks back. Here's some of what we had to say about the record:

Moon Duo return with their second proper full length, and if you

needed any further proof of their masterful grasp of fuzzed out

hypnotic grooves, well, look no further. It's almost as if Moon Duo

set out to link every one of their records together as one gigantic

pulsating trip into your mind, and Mazes takes you further in. Mazes.

Hmm. Like the magic eye cover for their Catch As Catch Can single, the

eyeball warping artwork seems to be a perfect visual accompaniment to

the awesome brain-melting confusion of the music itself.

Kicking off with "Seer", the duo conjure the hazy underwater sound

they've utilized before with a rhythm that walks the line between

spaced out acid rock and minimal electro grooves with some of the

clearest Moon Duo vocals to date. "Mazes" offers a slight departure

from the usual murk, sounding poppy and jangly in the vein of other

San Francisco popsmiths like the Fresh And Onlys or the Mantles. It

rides out with a sort of pre-1965 surf rock feel that makes this a

perfect jam for your next sun baked road trip. "Scars" shifts the mood

into darker terrain like some fractured Suicide / Doors hybrid. The

heavy vibrato keeps things percolating while the organ occasionally

shifts into a spooky little interlude recalling Kraftwerk's "The

Model". The song also strangely makes us think of Steppenwolf's "Born

To Be Wild", an association which is taken to the next logical step

with the aggressive "Fallout". The album concludes with "Goners", a

midtempo jam that makes it feel like you are walking into the perfect

fadeout to an album which will remain playing in your head nonstop for

quite some time.

They'll be playing that same night at Bottom Of The Hill, with another

bunch of aQ faves Royal Baths, so definitely don't miss that. But why

not come down to aQ first and enjoy a super special, extra intimate

Moon Duo set, to whet your appetite for the rocking to come...
