Beloved Customers and Friends:

Hey hey hey, Record Store Day is (almost) upon us! As you hopefully

already know, TOMORROW, Saturday April 18th, is the big day, the 8th

International Record Store Day.

What that is, is the day we all celebrate our favorite local (or not

so local) record stores. If you're reading this, hopefully that means

aQuarius is one of yours. Here at aQ, we expect a busy day of folks

coming down to shop, hang out, listen to tunes, drink some beer (in

the afternoon), and of course snag some of the special Record Store

Day releases.

This year, we're going to be opening early, at 8am (originally we'd

hoped to open at midnight, but that didn't work out). So those of you

who like to wait in line had better get up early (you should probably

be in bed already, like we aren't). We'll have donuts and coffee for

you early birds. When we open, we'll let you in the shop in batches of

like, 20, so it doesn't get too crazy crowded all at once. And note:

all the RSD items are in the center bins in front of the counter, with

7"s and box sets and stuff displayed on the front counter itself. Just

so you know where to look.

But remember, it's not ALL about the Record Store Day releases - we

mean, not only will we have plenty of those in stock, we've of course

got lots of other great things for sale here too, so if you can't make

it in the morning, come by later on in the day. Sure those Metallica

"No Life 'Til Leather" cassettes might be gone by then, but you'll

find something cool regardless, and we'll be making it a party with

free beers and snacks and so forth.

However, if you are wondering just which RSD releases we will have

tomorrow morning, we're providing a list, below. You can't order this

stuff, though, not yet - but it's what we'll have in the shop

tomorrow, when you come by - if you

get here early enough, that is. But, be warned, as you probably

expect, some of these releases are SO insanely limited, we could only

get a handful, in a lot of cases, a mere fraction of what we tried to

order. So please don't get your heart set on just one thing and one

thing only. We did our best to get as many of the things we thought

you'd want as we could, but much is beyond our control. That said, we

did manage to pull strings to get an extra amount of a couple

particularly "AQ" titles, so we have a good supply of The Heads lp for

instance, and the Doug Hream Blunt remix 12" too! Among others.

And, huzzah, there are a few AQ EXCLUSIVE items as well you need to

know about!!

There's the limited edition cd reissue of the debut lp "Eyewash

Silver" by one-man blown out hypno-boogie distortodelic rock band KING

BLOOD, complete with 3 bonus tracks, that we ourselves have put out

for this year's Record Store Day (3rd in a series of exclusive

available-only-at-aQ RSD releases we've done, following discs by White

Hills and Carlton Melton in previous years).

There's also a brand new AQ tote bag, too!

Both are listed below, with our descriptions, and unlike the other RSD

stuff, if you're a mailorder customer you can put your orders in for

'em now and we'll make sure you get them.

Now, about the other RSD stuff. Again, you can scroll down a find a

list of what we got. Some things we only got 2 or 3 of, others a

dozen, or more. It depends. One of the things on there we got a bunch

of is another aQ exclusive, a cd-r from our pals local psych band 3

Leafs (thanks guys!).

For those of you who aren't local and/or can't make it down here

tomorrow for whatever reason, here's the deal.

Firstly, for all you far-flung mailorder customers who want some of

the Record Store Day goodies, take a look at the things on this in-

between list and feel free to email and ask us to grab stuff you want

for you, which we will do - IF we have any left after tomorrow. Ok?

We'll pull orders Sunday morning for RSD

leftovers. Sorry but that's how we have to do it, them's the official

Record Store Day rules. Also, there will most likely be a few Record

Store Day releases that we got enough of to actually review and list

on our next New Arrivals update (#473), so keep an eye out for that

next Friday!

Another note for mailorder folks - if you place an order or email us

tomorrow, regarding RSD stuff or regular mailorder business or

whatever, please don't worry if we don't get back to you as quickly as

we usually do - we'll be super busy up front in the shop all day as

you can imagine and probably won't be answering any emails 'til

Sunday. Thanks!

If you're local, and can't make it tomorrow 'cause of a wedding or

something, well, come by Sunday, chances are there will be a bunch of

great RSD stuff still in the bins.

Ok, that's about it, better get this emailed so you can see it before

tomorrow! And we've still got more to do to get everything ready...

oh, a shout out/big thanks to our 'volunteer intern' Claire for all

her help this evening!! And MC too!

Have a great Record Store Day wherever you are, but if you're here in

town, hopefully we can celebrate together! See ya!!

(To view this in-betweener on our website, please go to http://www.aquariusrecords.org/cat/4725.html)

----* Our aQ Record Store Day 2015 Exclusive Compact Disc Release :

KING BLOOD "Eyewash Silver" (aQuarius recOrds) cd 12.98

Third in the series of special aQ Record Store Day limited

edition cd releases we've put out ourselves! We skipped last year, but

in 2012 we did a White Hills cd, and in 2013 a Carlton Melton disc

(both long out of print). Now, we're back with a RSD cd for 2015, from

our favorite lo-fi, distorto boogie, zoner-psych one man band, King

Blood! It's a cd reissue of King Blood's debut album originally

released in 2010/11 on vinyl in two tiny pressings via Ignorant Gore

and then Permanent Records, both out of print. So now we're stoked to

be doing the cd version - with three bonus tracks!! Here's what we

wrote about it before:

Long time readers of the aQ list no doubt remember Tetuzi

Akiyama's Don't Forget To Boogie record, ostensibly, as the title

suggests, a boogie rock record, but in reality, a twisted droned out

hypnotic psychedelic minimal psych record, with the boogie elements

stripped down to a single riff, then drenched in distortion and

repeated over and over and over, creating super mesmerizing fields of

layered repetition a la Reich or Riley, it quickly became a bigtime aQ

favorite.

This debut lp from one man 'boogie' outfit King Blood,

approaches boogie rock in a similarly oblique fashion, taking those

boogie riffs and fucking them up big time. Not quite as minimal as

Akiyama's opus, Eyewash Silver adds drums, and what sounds like

multiple layers of guitars, not to mention WAY more distortion, a

gloriously blown out chunk of experimental abstract minimal

boogienoise weirdness.

Opener â€œBlack Moneyâ€� is probably the most traditionally song-y,

a loping lumbering midtempo groove, a main riff locked tight and

looped over a similarly tight barely there rhythm, but then all around

it, guitars howl and grind, churn and crunch, loads of distortion and

tangled melodies, swirling clouds of corrosive sound wrapped around

that fantastically incessant groove, gradually growing more and more

explosive, before the noise guitars nearly swallow up the boogie riff

completely.

â€œEnd Of A Primitiveâ€� sounds like a Stooges sample dipped in

distortion and left outside to crumble and decay before being brought

back in and recorded onto some 20 year old 2â€� tape that had been

the song gradually shifting, but so gradually, it's easy to get

twang flecked dirge, like an echo drenched Earth, via the desert rock

Limited to only 300 copies, all hand numbered, available

two for us to reply. We are not a faceless bunch of computers replying

within the same week, we can wait to ship. Or... If it's going to be

more than a few days to complete your order, we will ship what we have

and then will contact you as the remainders arrive.

business, we are not responsible for listed price changes (due to

supplier price changes) and often cannot update our site fast enough

to reflect these changes, but we will always try to let you know of

any differences.

INTERNATIONAL INSURANCE :

-------------------------------- You are hereby forewarned that

Aquarius is not responsible if your international package gets lost in

the mail. Insurance is your only recourse if your records never show

up. We very rarely experience *confirmed* permanently lost mail, but

insurance might provide some additional piece of mind. We strongly

recommend it. But yes, it is very expensive. Even moreso recently with

the recent hike in USPS international shipping rates, but still, it's

your choice. Again: Aquarius is not responsible for lost mail, so if

you aren't willing to take a (slight but real) risk, please buy the

insurance.

International insurance is very expensive! In fact often the insurance

costs more than the value of your package, in which case it obviously

does not make sense to insure it. You can check the US Postal Service

international rate calculator: http://ircalc.usps.gov/. (Use the

"Package" category and see the price for "Priority Mail

International", which is the way insured packages are sent. 1-3 cds is

usually 1 pound. 1 lp is usually a between 1.25 and 1.5 pounds.)

For example: for a one-pound package worth $25 going to England,

shipping without insurance is about $16.75. But with insurance, the

shipping / insurance total is over $42! Go up to two pounds and

uninsured first class is about $24, insured priority over $46. But

bigger packages weighing over four pounds automatically get bumped up

to the priority rate, in which case insurance is generally only a few

dollars more and in that case definitely worth it.

It is your reponsibility to check the international rate calculator in

order to determine whether or not you want international insurance. If

you tell us you want international insurance, we will add it to your

order no matter how much it costs!
