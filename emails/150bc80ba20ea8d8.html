----* Torch Of The Mystics Reissued! :

----*

SUN CITY GIRLS "Torch Of The Mystics" (Abduction) cd/lp 15.98/19.98

Space Prophet Dogon has returned!!! Along with all the other alternate-universe 'hits' on this fantastic album, like "Blue Mambo" and "Esoterica Of Abyssynia", reissued now on vinyl for the first time since it was originally released 25 years ago - and on cd too, which has also out of print forever. To say we've been waiting for this is an understatement. We love Sun City Girls, and Torch Of The Mystics is not-so-arguably acclaimed as their most enjoyable, and certainly most accessible album. THE one to have, or to start with. Not that it's not really weird, it is... but compared so a lot of SCG stuff, it's practically mainstream.

For most of us here, it's also not just our favorite SCG album, but really of our favorite albums EVER - though it's been out of print and unavailable for far too long; in fact, there's at least one longtime aQ staffer who had NEVER heard it before, we're excited for them (and you're not alone out there if you've never heard it either). So at long last we're able to stock it again and urge you to buy one if you don't have it - or get another if you do!

Reviewing anything this beloved is tough, sometimes we just wanna say something like this: "Greatest band of all time, and possibly their greatest record, we don't even know what to say about it! It's really good, buy it! Mind will be blown. We're out..." There's our review, yep. But don't worry, if you really want a review, we'll come up with something more next week. It'll surely involve discussion of the surfy-skronky guitar, the memorable ethnic-folk hookiness, the instrumental mastery, the beauty and mystery and sheer exuberance of the album. A lot of Torch Of The Mystics was inspired by music the Sun City Girls encountered while traveling in Southeast Asia and other far-flung, foreign locales, and in a way this doesn't seem as strange and alien now as it once did, as that strain of international, third-world folk & pop music has gotten a lot more more exposure over the years since this first came out, thanks in large part to the efforts of labels like Sublime Frequencies, which is run by of Alan Bishop of Sun City Girls of course. Take the whole Sublime Frequencies catalog, and mix in a little Meat Puppets and some of Greg Ginn's Gone, maybe that's an approximation of what the Sun City Girls were up to on Torch Of The Mystics, recorded back in 1988 and originally released on lp via Majora in 1990 (note: this remastered reissue bears the same art, on both lp and digipack cd, as did that Majora edition).

So look for our 'real' review next week, possibly expanded from this one, but we figured since it came out TODAY we should not delay in getting it up on our website for everyone who has been waiting for it. So it gets its very own "in-between list", but we'll be listing it again on next week's New Arrivals, of course, where it'll be Record Of The Week for sure!

INTERNATIONAL INSURANCE :

-------------------------------- You are hereby forewarned that Aquarius is not responsible if your international package gets lost in the mail. Insurance is your only recourse if your records never show up. We very rarely experience *confirmed* permanently lost mail, but insurance might provide some additional piece of mind. We strongly recommend it. But yes, it is very expensive. Even moreso recently with the recent hike in USPS international shipping rates, but still, it's your choice. Again: Aquarius is not responsible for lost mail, so if you aren't willing to take a (slight but real) risk, please buy the insurance.

International insurance is very expensive! In fact often the insurance costs more than the value of your package, in which case it obviously does not make sense to insure it. You can check the US Postal Service international rate calculator: http://ircalc.usps.gov/. (Use the "Package" category and see the price for "Priority Mail International", which is the way insured packages are sent. 1-3 cds is usually 1 pound. 1 lp is usually a between 1.25 and 1.5 pounds.)

For example: for a one-pound package worth $25 going to England, shipping without insurance is about $16.75. But with insurance, the shipping / insurance total is over $42! Go up to two pounds and uninsured first class is about $24, insured priority over $46. But bigger packages weighing over four pounds automatically get bumped up to the priority rate, in which case insurance is generally only a few dollars more and in that case definitely worth it.

It is your responsibility to check the international rate calculator in order to determine whether or not you want international insurance. If you tell us you want international insurance, we will add it to your order no matter how much it costs!
