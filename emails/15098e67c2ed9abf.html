BLUNT, DOUG HREAM "My Name Is Doug Hream Blunt" (Luaka Bop) cd/lp 16.98/21.00

We can't believe it's been five years since Doug Hream Blunt himself walked into our store and consigned his self-released recordings of home-brewed, proto-chillwave dance funk, recorded with washy and chirpy synths and genuinely out there guitar solos, with songs about dancing, fly guys and ladies' butts. Kind of a smoother Wesley Willis (ok, much smoother!) with Jimi Hendrix dreams, Blunt's genuinely infectious musical enthusiasm shined through on each track. We made Gentle Persuasion record of the week back in 2010, without knowing much about its backstory. Doug was very vague on details too, saying the recording was from 2000, but we believe that was when it was pressed, but not recorded. Having suffered a stroke a few years before, he couldn't go into great length about the recording at the time (he's doing much better now, as evidenced by our recent record signing event!), but we had an inkling that the recordings were known in certain crate-digger / DJ circles, which were immediately confirmed once we had it on our website and orders from all over the world started pouring in.

Seriously, we have sold tons of his release, and now lo and behold, Doug is finally having his well-deserved, big moment of larger recognition and discovery via David Byrne's Luaka Bop label, with this reissue of his complete recordings which include the two previously self-released discs we previously listed: Gentle Persuasion and Big Top, remastered and put together for the first time - these tracks have never sounded better! A lo-fi but breezy outsider funk, made with repetitive cheap-sounding keyboards loops and tones (the kind being currently exploited by Dam-Funk, Ariel Pink, and James Pants, all Doug Hream Blunt fans by the way), strange metallic tropical guitar tones that sound more like steel drums and flute sounds than actual guitar, and sly, laidback vocals and catchy, infectiously naive grooves that work their way into our heads and never let go. So delightful!!

And we finally get more of the story behind these recordings: Back in 1985, when Doug was 35, he decided he wanted to learn guitar and found an extended learning class called 'How To Form A Band' run by Victor Flaviani out of his garage. Victor taught Doug the guitar, even though Victor barely knew the instrument himself, and Doug set about writing and recording the songs with his other class mates. We remember seeing the video of Gentle Persuasion we found on Vimeo from a cable access TV video with Doug and what seemed to be an all-girl band, which turns out were his fellow classmates, which included Victor and his wife Angela too on drums and guitar respectively.

It's pretty phenomenal that this record went from getting the suspicious side-eye when we first took it in on consignment, five years ago, to becoming one of our favorite "WTF?" records ever. Not that it's so impossibly strange, but it's a genuine home-grown San Francisco DIY funk artifact with minimal ambitions that is finally being turned on to a larger audience, and we couldn't be happier for Doug! For fans of that Personal Space compilation of private press electronic soul, Dam-Funk, Ariel Pink or Dean Blunt, who apparently took the Blunt name in tribute to Doug!

Download code included with the vinyl btw. Also btw, if you missed the remix 12" that Luaka Bop put out as a teaser for this earlier this year for Record Store Day, we still have three or four signed copies of that left in stock...

DEPARTMENTSTORE SANTAS "At The Medieval Castle Nineteen 100-Year Lifetimes Since" (Superior Viaduct) lp 17.98

If Martin Newell from Cleaners From Venus or Robert Pollard of Guided By Voices somehow grew up in the sleepy San Diego suburb of La Mesa, CA in the seventies, managed to secure a bunch of instruments and recording equipment from garage sales and swap meets and then recorded in their bedroom an elegiac ode to the ordinary charm and civic pride of their childhood hometown, you would get something very close to this brilliantly lo-fi slab of DIY bizarro-pop wondrousness!

Recorded in 1984 by Joseph D'Angelo and a handful of friends, At The Medieval Castle Nineteen 100-Year Lifetimes Since (one of the best titles ever!), is the kind of obscure scrappy visionary recording (along with the likes of Daniel Johnston, Sun City Girls and The Frogs) that is embedded in the DNA of so many indie bands that came afterward - from Yo La Tengo and The Supreme Dicks to The Unicorns and the bands of The Elephant 6 collective. It's a hotly Technicolor teenage daydream of nostalgic childhood, of Sears Christmas catalogs, Disneyland, skate parks and summer carnivals, that is slowly fading away, as some songs here delve into more ornery young adult concerns, like the psychedelic pseudo anti-drug PSA of "An Open Letter To The Seniors From Mackenzie". Layered riffs of guitar, horns, organs and drum machines, sometimes used in pondering twee instrumentals, but more often in wide-eyed and wobbly surreal psych-pop confections, seemingly sung by earnest but very warped high schoolers. In our minds we can imagine Departmentstore Santas trying to enter this record in a city wide talent contest sponsored by the local chamber of commerce and barely squeaking by with an honorable mention from a baffled jury. So very great and weird!! Thanks to the folks at Superior Viaduct for digging out this obscurity. A must have for fans of any of the above bands/artists mentioned!

FUZZ "II" (In The Red) cd/2lp 13.98/24.00

Yowza. Hells to the yeah. It's Fuzz II time. If'n you don't know, Fuzz is the stomping heavy psych power trio featuring garage pop wunderkind Ty Segall (playing the flailin' drums) as well as the Ty Segall Band's Charlie Moothart, wailin' on the guitar, and rounding out the trio, Chad from The Meatbodies is now their bassist. After a couple brilliant singles, Fuzz released their eponymous debut in 2013, blowing us away and earning Record Of The Week honors at aQ. Now they're back... with another ROTW, natch!

Fuzz II means the next level of Fuzz. Advanced Fuzz. Definitely more Fuzz - there's 14 tracks here, some short blasts, but others mega jams - including a lengthy, 13+ minute album-ending epic freakout that is apparently titled "II" (yes, a numbered album with a title track, brilliant!).

Of course, if you call your band Fuzz, that's what you gotta deliver. And they sure do, job number one. Lots and lots of fuzz, check. Moothart again channels the likes Leigh Stephens, Randy Holden, Martin Weaver, and other legendary axe-wielding fuzzmasters of yesteryear. Ye olde stoner rawkin' Blue Cheery, Black Sabbathy heaviness is a big part of the Fuzz equation, as are the garage psych pop smarts Segall is known for on all his other records. For us, it's a killer combination!! And this time 'round their longhaired retro-proto-metal riffage is maybe a mite more proggy and complexified, more Sir Lord Baltimore baroque than Blue Cheer basic. Well, they still turn the air into cottage cheese, but there's a whiff of some fancy camembert in there too, with a couple tracks bringing in some orchestrated sounds (must be by the guest credited with "InterstellarStringSweeping"). And there's keys and synths and stuff too. Not that it's nothing but muscular hard-hitting rock action though, sounding like some lost late '60s / early '70s classic that just couldn't be, 'cause it's so grindingly badass and freshly in your melted face.

We're digging it big time, all the freaky sounds and hooks and riffs and FUZZ. Ty Segall never lets us down, and as serious heavy psych lovers, Fuzz is one of our favorite projects of his. For fans of all its proto-metal inspirations like Sir Lord Baltimore, as well as the music of Ty's pal King Tuff, and even moreso, King Tuff-related band Witch.

AEVANGELIST "Enthrall To The Void Of Bliss" (20 Buck Spin) cd 13.98

We planned on reviewing this for our last list, but got a little slammed and forgot, oops! But no matter, because in the meantime, we managed to catch an Aevangelist live set at the first incarnation of California Deathfest the other day! And boy, was it ridiculous. Already we were convinced that Aevangelist was one of thee most bizzare, psychotic bands we have heard recently, but seeing 'em live totally confirmed it! THREE guitarists, one looking like some lost member of Coal Chamber, a keyboardist, and a saxophonist (and, yeah, bass and drums too). It perfectly translated the songs on this record into a live experience, a huge warped wall of sound, sometimes totally metal, other times just a psychedelic mass of chaos.

So we come to the record itself: Enthrall To The Void Of Bliss is a twisted mass of psychotic riffage and insanity-inducing atmospherics, total musical madness and sonic depravity. Weird vocals that have no problem shifting between out-and-out slamz style gutturals, black metal shrieking, and operatic howls. Sometimes the songs have so much happening at once, it almost feels like two totally different songs playing simultaneously. Samples, organ, some sort of harp instrument, and saxophone combine with the already massive sounding guitars and drums and vocals to create an even bigger, grosser wall of sound. Take the dense and lysergic doom-laden death and black riffage of some of those recent Ajna Offensive bands like Saturnalia Temple and Aluk Todolo and combine them with the total WTF? weirdness of Sigh and throw in some seriously hateful riffing of Nuclear War Now! style war metal and you're sorta getting the idea here. You also get songs like "Alchemy" and "Meditation of Transcendental Evil" which totally come out of nowhere, being witch-housey, programmed-drum numbers that may not sound like the other tracks on here but somehow still completely fit in to the total carnival-like madness. Totally a must-listen for fans of Gnaw Their Tongues, Mitochondrian, Saturnalia Temple, Sigh, and anything on Crucial Blast.

BEACH HOUSE "Thank Your Lucky Stars" (Sub Pop) lp 17.98

"Come a little closer," Victoria Legrand croons on "All Your Yeahs", inviting us to lean in to Beach House’s new (nearly surprise) release, Thank Your Lucky Stars. Trailing their last album by only a handful of weeks, really, it’d be easy to see Thank Your Lucky Stars as no more than a sequel to the highly-hyped, velvet-coated opus that was Depression Cherry. However, Thank Your Lucky Stars stands on its own. Its shoegazey guitar solos weave their way through warm synth melodies (notably on "One Thing") and Legrand’s vocals shine through more clearly than on past releases. This album is a bit of a darker and heavier take on their style of twinkly, extra-terrestrial style of dream pop. The weight can be felt most on this album’s twisted and tortured B-side tracks "Rough Song" and "Elegy to the Void". But, with the perfect amount of lo-fi fuzz and room to breathe between each song, we’re given the time to appreciate how simply beautiful this album is. Every other Beach House release has felt like the soundtrack to a loving embrace. Thank Your Lucky Stars reaches out to us as honest reassurance on the nights where we feel alone remind us that, though we are each singular stars, we reside in a sky populated with billions of constellations.

(Compact disc version forthcoming, but this was such a rush release they didn't get it done at the same time as the vinyl.)

BELZEBONG "Greenferno" (Emetic) cd/lp 13.98/21.00

Greenferno is the latest cloud of sickly sweet, Satanic, sonic bongsmoke from these Polish stoner heavies, and if the band name, and the album name, and the song titles (“Diabolical Dopenosis”, “Inhale In Hell”, “Undertoker”, and “Goat Smokin Blues”) didn’t clue you in, these guys traffic in wasted riffage, massive Sabbathy stoner sludge, which as always, owes much to all the other bong-bands that came before (Bong Ripper, Bongzilla, and, er, Bong!), but these guys are HEAVY, and have some serious swagger and swing. It’s tough to pull of instrumental music without being boring, but somehow, these extended jams are totally hypnotic, and super dynamic, lumbering from churning doomic crush, to more laid back, psychedelic slither. There are some stoner appropriate samples here and there of course, but really, it’s about the RIFFS, and the vibe, and these guys are doped out masters. Every track here (the shortest clocking in at a still respectable 6 minutes) delivers bleary eared blasts of blown out heaviosity, wild shredding, FX drenched leads, epic drum pound, all of it utterly headbangable, but also head noddingly drugged-out. We consider ourselves pretty serious connoisseurs of stoner metal, even the non stoners among us (maybe ESPECIALLY the non-stoners), and these guys are definitely amongst our favorite sonic bong slingers. Totally recommended for those jonesing for some seriously wasted, skull crushing, mind expanding, stoner bliss!

BERBER OX "Communication With The Interior" (Cave Recordings) cassette 5.98

Latest transmission of mysterious, hazy sonics from this longtime aQ fave, a super limited tape that once again demonstrates Berber Ox’s ability to wring pathos and emotion from field recordings and sculpted soft noise.

This time around, things start with a sprawl of grey crumble, and gradually, from within this cloud of sound surfaces the sounds of what appears to be the seaside? Birds, voices, the occasional horn, children laughing... The vibe is very tranquil, aside from the strange muffled voice, that is seemingly projected from an old loudspeaker, but really, the whole thing is transformed by the ambient cloud wreathing those disembodied sounds. Subtle melodies surface through, so subtle in fact, that they almost seem like mirages. It definitely requires attentive listening, and some headphones, but the rewards are great, it’s easy to get deeply lost in this deceptively simple arrangement of sound.

The rest of the tape is equally beguiling - muted thrums, softly psychedelic tones, processed sounds mutated into smears and blurs, buried percussion constructed from sonic detritus, never quite allowed to coalesce into a proper rhythms, instead, used to add texture and timbre, occasionally slipping into lovely minimal drifts, while often seeming to dissolve into shapeless streaks of monochromatic sound, usually grey sky-ing some sonic snippet of everyday life, overcast, haunting, melancholy, and utterly mesmerizing.

Nicely packaged and extremely limited. We got these direct from the label, and while we will hopefully be able to get more, it’s certainly possible that these are the last copies we’ll see.

BUTTHOLE SURFERS "Brown Reason To Live" (Alternative Tentacles) lp 13.98

NOW AT LAST REPRESSED! We last listed this like six years ago, finally have 'em back in...

The debut recording by these legendary Texan drug punks, finally available again on vinyl! Brown Reason To live was the Surfers' first record, a seven song ep, originally released in 1983, and reissued later bundled onto a cd with their live PCPPEP, but as good as that live stuff was/is, these tracks all on their own constitute one of THEE legendary punk rock records EVER. And we say punk rock knowing full well that the Buttholes' version of punk rock had almost nothing in common with what the rest of the world at the time considered punk.

These songs are damaged and disturbing, wild and chaotic, extended stumbling lysergic blowouts, as likely to erupt into a strange woozy sea shanty or a bit of twangy gallop as a blown out blast of pounding punk, moreso really. And while the sound here is not as overtly whatthefuck as the records that would come right after, it's weird enough that you could tell something freaky was going on, and you can hear the seeds that would blossom into that the Surfers eventually became. The Buttholes merged the punk sounds that surrounded them, with their own twisted take on DIY music making, not to mention lots and lots and lots and lots of drugs. All of these songs are timeless, total classics, but "The Shah Sleeps In Lee Harvey's Grave" is practically a punk rock anthem, or no less than the genre's swan song. Depending on how you look at it, maybe both. For the band anyway, the track was every bit a standard as "Free Bird" was for Skynyrd. Listening to this again now, it seems insane that a band like this - who at one time seemed destined to a life of cult obscurity - achieved such enormous mainstream success. It's all urine under the bridge now though, we try to ignore the last few releases and focus on the years when the Butthole Surfers were untouchable ('83-'88?), and this is where it all started, a ferocious fucked up chunk of drug addled genius called Brown Reason To Live.

FYI Alternative Tentacles have also just reissued the Live PCPPEP on vinyl again as well!

CHRISTIAN MISTRESS "To Your Death" (Relapse) cd/lp 14.98/22.00

Finally these celebrated Portland, Oregon heavy metal heroes (and heroine!!!) are BACK, with their long awaited third opus. We've been huge fans of the band since their debut album, Agony & Opium, which in fact we made a Record Of The Week in 2010, and then 2012's Possession also had us slobbering. But that was three long years ago, not sure what happened to slow Christian Mistress's seemingly considerable momentum at the time, we know they had some line up issues, and also a weird dispute with Kanye West bizarrely enough (he stole their logo or something, WTF?), but anyway we're glad that at last they're rolling and rocking again! Saw 'em a couple months back playing down the road at The Elbo Room and they were on fire, sounding as good as ever which is saying a lot 'cause they've always ripped. Furthermore, their not-so-secret weapon, and crucial X-factor, the husky, extra special voice of Christine Davis, was soooo nice to hear again. The new songs they played had us even more excited about the impending album, here is is, and yeah, great stuff! Typically for the band, it's very classic '70s/'80s styled metal, speedy when they wanna be but often chugging along at a midpaced lope, allowing Davis to shine, she's in top form here for sure. Soaring melodies, shredding solos, headbanging riffs, a vibe that's triumphant and majestically emotive - even some acoustic guitars and moments for you to flick your lighter in the air...

Heck, we've raved about them so much before, do we need to add a lot more? Should be sufficient to say that they haven't lost a step, To Your Death delivers the traditional, well-crafted, memorably melodic heavy METAL that we expect from Christian Mistress, thank the gods! If you dig real old school sounding bands like High Spirits and Slough Feg and you don't have all three Christian Mistress albums in your collection, get with the program, poser!!

COIL "Backwards" (Cold Spring) cd 17.98

There's undoubtably a secret history to this album, one that will never be known as the founding / principle members John Balance and Peter Christopherson have both died in the past decade. Backwards was the Coil album that was slated for release sometime in the mid-to-late '90s on Trent Reznor's Nothing imprint, with various declarations of its pending release sporadically offered as a monumental tease to Coil's fanbase. To track the history a little bit: Coil released their groundbreaking album Love's Secret Domain in 1992 and began crafting the tracks for Backwards during a period of searching that took Balance & Christopherson to New Orleans back to England and finally onto Bangkok. Whilst in New Orleans, Coil (then fleshed out with Danny Hyde and Drew McDowell) recorded a what would become the Backwards album; but for reasons that always seemed opaque, the tapes sat dormant. Lawyers at Interscope and Universal could be to blame for the fact that Coil refused to deliver the tapes. Some fifteen years later, Christopherson would remix those tapes into The New Backwards, an album that represented his final vision of what that material could be; and some of those songs and tracks had made their way onto other Coil albums and as compilation contributions in some remixed form or another. But finally, FINALLY, there's now the posthumous release of Coil's Backwards, engineered into this current form by then Coil engineer / member Danny Hyde.

If anything, Coil were a baroque proposition; and Backwards is very much a baroque album, with a kitchen sink of electronics and live instrumentation liberally saturated throughout the cryptically serpentine synth-pop sensibility that made Coil such a unique band. Skittering, static pulse sequences formed the basis to much of Backwards, alternating between darkly lysergic dance numbers and ballast-heavy marches of somber, twisted, and / or malignant thought. The titular track is certainly a standout with Balance chanting amidst scurrying, hectic collages, countered by the swaggering hip-sway of the rhythm track - very much in keeping with what Coil had done on Love's Secret Domain. The immediate next track "Amber Rain", with its impassioned vocal hymn from Balance and an melancholy melody for synthetic piano, looks forward to what Coil would later do on Musick To Play In The Dark. Hindsight is 20-20, and of course, this album has been claimed to be - and should be seen - as the transition between those two landmark works by Coil. The original version of "Heaven's Blade" did get a much better makeover on the Ape Of Naples in 2005, but here it stands as a proto drill'n'bass number of compressed breakbeats and a splattered tapestry of occluded electronics amidst Balance's vocals. Many more rich, dark, and sordid details await on Backwards - a very welcome entry in the already hallowed Coil canon.

DE LEON, MARIO DIAZ "The Soul Is The Arena" (Denovali) cd 16.98

We've reviewed (and raved about!!) composer Mario Diaz De Leon's music before, but those were always his projects for electronics and guitars, a kind of dronedoom minimalism meets synthed-out psychedelic planetarium show music, heavy and hypnotic (one of our faves being his album Hypnos, which we made a Record Of The Week). But, De Leon is also a composer for larger ensembles, in somewhat more of an academic chamber music mold. He had a great album of such compositions on John Zorn's Tzadik label a while back, and now this labyrinth-themed one (twists and turns and inner discovery) on Denovali, featuring three pieces commissioned by the International Contemporary Ensemble, who perform these works.

The first two tracks both feature electronic Morse code bursts of static & distortion interjected amidst more typically orchestral, melodic (but sometimes equally maniacal) instrumentation. The first one, "Luciform", is for flute and electronics; the second, "The Soul Is The Arena", for bass clarinet and electronics, and both at times sound like some eerie '60s sci-fi soundtrack, such as Alexander Courage's Star Trek music, but overrun by drone-snippets of video-game glitch and buzz-blurt, all of it carefully/crazily patterned and ordered. It can be a bit dizzying and disorienting, in a good way, but often pretty and pleasant too.

Then, the third track, "Portals Before Dawn" is the disc's big finale, a slowly unfurling twenty minutes of many moods, performed by the entire Ensemble, with flutes, clarinets, piano and percussion, alongside De Leon's synths. He's woven together a genuine epic, at turns blissfully mesmeric, eerily suspenseful, dense and dramatic, and gorgeously majestic. Bravo!

While it's already established that Mario Diaz De Leon can make great albums entirely on his own, we do hope he gets more opportunities/commissions to work with ensembles like this in the future, 'cause while it's not so rare to hear someone doing cool stuff with synths, you don't get to hear that juxtaposed with the various chamber music textures heard here, nearly as often.

DEATH AND VANILLA "California Owls" (Fire) 12" 25.00

A marvelous haunted-pop ep from Sweden's' Death & Vanilla, who reprises two of the best tracks on their 2015 album To Where The Wild Things Are. The titular track and "Follow The Light" are blithe tracks of bachelor pad psychedelic poptunes with a soft focus of sexy spookiness rendered through mellotrons and sampled harpsichords accompanying the tremolo fuzzed guitars and the breathy delivery from Death & Vanilla's chanteuse Marleen Nilsson. Two unreleased tracks find their way onto the B-side with a radiophonic track of Derbyshire-ish analogue percolations leading into a triumphantly cinematic number that should make Morricone stand up and take notice. A repeating motif for a wordlessly vocalized melody builds through an groovy piece of avant-pop tension, only to collapse into a swirling melancholy where Nilsson's soft focus voice and an acoustic guitar are the lone protagonists amidst all of Death & Vanilla's vibrato trills and tremolo frills. As we said, marvelous!

DRAGONTIME "s/t" (Inner Islands) cassette 4.98

We first listed this pop oddity as a cd-r on Matt Baldwin's Psychic Arts label, now another local label, Inner Islands, has reissued this on cassette!

Dragontime, a group from Oakland, features some cool psychedelic Rainbow Brite style paste on cover art, that definitely points to something a bit on the weird side. And it's that artwork that maybe gives the biggest clue to what's going on here. KOSTIS, A "A Jail Is A Fine School" (Mississippi) lp 16.98

***MISSISSIPPI ALERT***MISSISSIPPI ALERT***MISSISSIPPI ALERT***

Another remarkable release from the Mississippi label, this time in conjunction with the newly formed Olvido imprint, run by Gordon Ashworth, prolific sound artist, member of aQ faves Knelt Rote, and who somehow also finds time to make music under Concern, Oscillating Innards, his own name, and probably more.

The Jail's A Fine School is a gorgeous collection of archival rebetika recordings made by the mysterious Constantine Bezos during the early 1930's. Dug up and mastered from the original 78s and assembled for the first time ever on lp, these recordings capture and chronicle the dark and seedy underbelly of old Athens. Bezos released these particular recordings under the pseudonyms A. Kostis and K. Kostis for reasons which aren't totally clear (although probably having to do with the reputation of early rebetika music in Greece as lower class music). Centered around guitar duets, which, according to the extensive liner notes, would later be seen as quite unusual in rebetika recordings, the bouzouki becoming the primary instrument of that once "disreputable" genre. The guitar melodies on here are on their own worth the price of admission, and there are a couple instrumentals that really showcase Bezos' expressive guitar playing style, but the gravelly croon of Bezos' voice is really the highlight of the record. Yeah, the lyrics are in Greek, but it doesn't really matter, and in fact, adds to the already mysterious nature of these recordings.

A totally unique and stunning release, unlike anything we've heard before (although we are admittedly not rebetiko experts). And, we couldn't possibly review this record without mentioning the massive booklet that comes with the lp! Info on Constantine Bezos' life, with extensive notes on rebetika music, the historical context of music during pre-WWII Greece, lyrics in both Greek and English, guitar tunings, previously unpublished photographs, and a glossary of some Greek slang used in the lyrics. Incredibly thorough, and just another reason to not sleep on this one.

MECANICA POPULAR "Que Sucede Con El Tiempo?" (Dead Cert) lp 24.00

The Dead Cert Label thankfully unearths this obscure global-futurist tape loop collage of industrial new wave rhythms and concrete fourth-world ambiance from 1984 Madrid. Mecanica Popular were Luis Delgado (later of Finis Africae), and Eugenio Munoz, who conceived this recording using only tape loops and an innovative setup featuring an Arp Odyssey, an Eventide H910 Harmonizer and a Polaroid 600 camera (!) fed through an array of effects to make intriguing mechanical rhythms and soundscapes that whirr and clank in intriguing combinations. MILK FROM CHELTENHAM "Triptych of Poisoners" (Superior Viaduct) lp 17.98

One of a handful of new releases this week from local label Superior Viaduct, a killer batch of legendary/infamous oddball rarities, elsewhere you can check out Departmentstore Santas, and Homosexuals side project L. Milk From Cheltenham set out to make their own Pet Sounds, but ended up with something far stranger, a damaged avant pop songsuite cobbled captured on an old cassette recorder, utilizing all manner of bits and bobs, cans and pieces of metal, boxes and veritable kitchen sink’s worth of junk-as-instruments, and maybe most hilariously, a primitive “surround” function on the tape recorder, which more than anything made the sound even LESS Pet Sounds and more gloriously WTF?

They’d also play the various sounds and recordings through big speakers and then re-record them, almost like a more primitive Taj Mahal Travellers!

It’s far out, and all over the map, but it’s also gleefully inventive, and a total fucking blast to get lost in. It’s not at all hard to understand why Triptych Of Poisoners was a crate digger holy grail, and thus was long, long overdue for a serious vinyl reissue!
