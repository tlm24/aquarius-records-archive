Beloved Customers and Friends:

Helllllloooo fellow music freaks! It's that time of the year, again,

wow. International RECORD STORE DAY is nigh - it's tomorrow, Saturday

April 19th, yeah!!

Local peeps, we hope (expect!) to see you here. It should be a ton of

fun, as always. Even though we we're goin' crazy right now getting

ready. You'd think having done this for several years now, we'd have a

routine down. And we do, but still somehow there's so much last minute

stuff to do! And stuff we can't really do until the last minute - like

wait 'til the store's closed tonight to put out all the Record Store

Day releases in our racks. And there's TONS of them. More on that in a

sec.

We open at 10am, as usual for Saturday (but this time we're gonna try

letting folks in batches, not all at once), and we'll have donuts for

the early birds, the folks who line up outside before we open (yep, if

you wanna be first in line, you gotta set yr alarm and get up early).

Then later in the afternoon, we'll have soda, beer, snacks. Party time!!

But of course, we know you're not coming by for the hors d'oeuvres.

Record Store Day means special limited Record Store Day releases

(well, that's not what it MEANS, to us, but it is one of the

significant parts of it). All records that you can only get at

participating indie record stores, like ours, starting tomorrow April

19th, while they last... which some will... but most won't.

So, for this Friday's in-between list, we're gonna let you know what

all we got (mostly). You can't order this stuff, though, not yet - but

it's what we'll have in the shop tomorrow, when you come by - if you

get here early enough, that is. But, be warned, as you probably

expect, some of these releases are SO insanely limited, we could only

get a handful, in a lot of cases, a mere fraction of what we tried to

order. So please don't get your heart set on just one thing and one

thing only. It doesn't help that even though more and more stores are

doing the Record Store Day thing, the labels don't generally

manufacture any more of each RSD item. In some cases, we ordered,

like, 25 or 30 copies of something, and received just 1. Grrrrrr.

(Those, we've left off this list - but they'll be in the racks, for at

least a second...)

That said, we will have LOTS of cool things, some in considerable

quantity. So we're confident you'll be able to pick up something

you'll like. And as we always say, what Record Store Day should really

be about, is celebrating the continued existence of good ol' brick and

mortar neighborhood record shops. And not just celebrating, but

helping to keep 'em going. (With that in mind, if you're not near us

in SF, we encourage you to visit YOUR local record store tomorrow, if

you're lucky enough to have one!)

So even if, say, the Bardo Pond release you wanted is gone (and sorry,

it will be, that's one of the ones they really should have made a lot

more of), hopefully you'll find something or somethings for sale here,

Record Store Day items or otherwise, that you'll be excited to

purchase to help the cause.

Of special note, are a few "aQ exclusives" we'll have for this year's

Record Store Day:

Firstly, VERY exclusive to aQuarius... people are always asking, now

we've finally got aQuarius tote bags!!!

It's a black bag, with silver print on one side of a brand new, 'black

metal' aQuarius Records logo, done by "the Lord of the Logos" himself,

the very talented (and super nice) Christophe Szpajdel. He did

Emperor's logo, among many others. He's the man. It's a nice lookin'

logo, kind of black metal meets art nouveau. The bags, printed by our

pals at Ape Do Good Printing, are big enough to hold a bunch of lps...

or cds, or books, or a few groceries for that matter. $11.98 a piece.

You can take a look at the bag here: http://www.aquariusrecords.org/aquatoteb.jpg

And another aQ RSD exclusive: The first in a series of limited 12"

vinyl releases by local heavy/space/prog/psych rock outfit TWIN

TRILOGY, lead by guitarist Sean Smith. They made 300 copies only,

exclusively for sale thru aQuarius for Record Store Day!

You can hear it here: https://twintrilogy.bandcamp.com/

They come in reclaimed hand painted/printed jackets, and include a

download.

And, Twin Trilogy will be playing a FREE INSTORE here on The Day After

Record Store Day, Sunday April 20th, at 4pm. Come check 'em out then!

And keep the RSD party goin' all weekend.

And not entirely exclusive, but mostly exclusive, and likely of major

interest to a lot of aQ customers - the long awaited vinyl reissues on

Thrill Jockey of the two albums by Andee's old (and now reunited,

about-to-go-on-tour) band, A MINOR FOREST. 1996's Flemish Altruism and

1998's Inindependence, both double lps, available together as a set at

a special price, packaged inside a limited edition tote bag -

featuring a new A Minor Forest logo also done by Christophe Szpajdel.

(See? They don't call him The Lord Of The Logos for nothin'.)

Also, the folks at Valley King records brought us the new picture disc

by NorCal stoner/psych heavies WHITE MANNA, letting us have it early

for Record Store Day. We'll also have the long overdue reissue of

SILKWORM's 1994 classic Libertine, released by aQ pal Jon Solomon's

Comedy Minus One label, early for RSD as well!

Beside those exclusive and semi-exclusives, there's lots more to be

excited about... you can see the full list on the aQ site here:

http://www.aquariusrecords.org/cat/4485.html

Or just scroll down and check it all out in this email.

For those of you who aren't local and/or can't make it down here

tomorrow for whatever reason, we don't want to leave you out, though!

Firstly, for all you far-flung mailorder customers who want some of

the Record Store Day goodies, here's the lowdown. Take a look at the

things on this in-between list and feel free to email and ask us to

grab stuff you want for you, which we will do - IF we have any left

after tomorrow. Ok? We'll pull orders Sunday morning for RSD

leftovers. Sorry but that's how we have to do it, them's the official

Record Store Day rules. Also, there will most likely be a few Record

Store Day releases that we got enough of to actually review and list

on our next New Arrivals update (#449), so keep an eye out for that

next Friday.

Another note for mailorder folks - if you place an order or email us

tomorrow, regarding RSD stuff or regular mailorder business or

whatever, please don't worry if we don't get back to you as quickly as

we usually do - we'll be super busy up front in the shop all day as

you can imagine and probably won't be answering any emails 'til

Sunday. Thanks!

And if you're local, and you come by Sunday or even later in the week,

there's always a chance we'll still have some of this stuff. Not to

mention the fact (which we kinda do like to mention) - we also always

have lots of amazing limited edition lp and cd and cassette etc.

releases here EVERY day, after all.

Another reason to come by on Sunday - that special instore performance

by Twin Trilogy mentioned above! And probably leftover beer from

Saturday too.

Whew! Ok, better wrap this up and get it sent out to y'all. We gotta

get back to moving racks and putting stuff out (and eating pizza and

arguing about the merits of sauce vs. no sauce, cheese only vs.

toppings, etc. etc.)

Thanks for tuning in (if you want to see last week's list proper, it's

HERE: http://www.aquariusrecords.org/cat/newest.html, still goin'

strong).

Hope to see you tomorrow - and Happy Record Store Day to you, wherever

you are!!

---------------------------------------------------------------

And as always, thanks for reading the list, passing it on to all your

friends who love weird music, shopping at our store, turning -us- on

to all sort of great stuff, and helping us spread the word and get all

this great music to the people who love it. YOU!! And as always,

please realize that we work really hard on the list, so if you find

out about stuff through us, please try to buy your records from us.

That way we can keep on doing what we do, and we'll always be here

with our ears to the ground, and with cds full of metalcore pitbulls,

death metal parrots, gamelan playing elephants, recordings of glaciers

cracking, ice melting, zamboni's, life support systems, drag races,

audience applause, and of course self flagellating Norwegian dwarves,

moaning telephone wires, recorded exorcisms, acapella straight edge

metalcore, high school battles of the bands, movie theater organ

music, Christian psychedelic folk, Bhangra Black Sabbath as well as

all the metal, indie rock, electronica, punk rock, reggae, dub,

sixties psych, krautrock, classic rock, country and anything else your

heart may desire. So thanks. A bunch!

Please place your order via our website.

[1] We will contact you to verify your order and let you know when it

will be shipped. Please note that occasionally it may take a day or

two for us to reply. We are not a faceless bunch of computers replying

to your order -- we are human beings!

[2] If we are out of some of your items and we think we will get them

within the same week, we can wait to ship. Or... If it's going to be

more than a few days to complete your order, we will ship what we have

and then will contact you as the remainders arrive.

[ note ] Due to the everchanging nature of the independent record

business, we are not responsible for listed price changes (due to

supplier price changes) and often cannot update our site fast enough

to reflect these changes, but we will always try to let you know of

any differences.

INTERNATIONAL SHIPPING :

-------------------------------- For foreign customers we ship via US

AIRMAIL ("First Class International"). Your price is based on the

actual cost of shipping. You can check the US Postal Service

international rate calculator: http://ircalc.usps.com/. (Use the

"Package" category and see the price for "First Class International".

pounds depending on the record)

We highly recommend insurance for your international package, but it

is very expensive (See below)! You can check the US Postal Service

international rate calculator: http://ircalc.usps.gov/. (In that case,

use the "Package" category and see the price for "Priority Mail

International". Again, 1-3 cds is usually 1 pound. 1 lp is usually

between 1.25 and 1.5 pounds.)

INTERNATIONAL INSURANCE :

-------------------------------- You are hereby forewarned that

Aquarius is not responsible if your international package gets lost in

the mail. Insurance is your only recourse if your records never show

up. We very rarely experience *confirmed* permanently lost mail, but

insurance might provide some additional piece of mind. We strongly

recommend it. But yes, it is very expensive. Even moreso recently with

the recent hike in USPS international shipping rates, but still, it's

your choice. Again: Aquarius is not responsible for lost mail, so if

you aren't willing to take a (slight but real) risk, please buy the

insurance.

International insurance is very expensive! In fact often the insurance

costs more than the value of your package, in which case it obviously

does not make sense to insure it. You can check the US Postal Service

international rate calculator: http://ircalc.usps.gov/. (Use the

"Package" category and see the price for "Priority Mail

International", which is the way insured packages are sent. 1-3 cds is

usually 1 pound. 1 lp is usually a between 1.25 and 1.5 pounds.)

For example: for a one-pound package worth $25 going to England,

shipping without insurance is about $16.75. But with insurance, the

shipping / insurance total is over $42! Go up to two pounds and

uninsured first class is about $24, insured priority over $46. But

bigger packages weighing over four pounds automatically get bumped up

to the priority rate, in which case insurance is generally only a few

dollars more and in that case definitely worth it.

It is your reponsibility to check the international rate calculator in

order to determine whether or not you want international insurance. If

you tell us you want international insurance, we will add it to your

order no matter how much it costs!
