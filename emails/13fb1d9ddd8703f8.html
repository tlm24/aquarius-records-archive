Beloved Customers and Friends:

Hey folks! Hope y'all had a happy, safe Independence Day, and a nice

week in general, whether you happen to be 'Murican like us or not.

Lots and lots of fireworks goin' off all around our neighborhood last

night, but despite all the explosions we still managed to get a whole

bunch of reviews done, so here's another wonderful New Arrivals list!

We've picked three Records Of The Week, all slightly twisted stuff,

new and old...

WILL OVER MATTER: Latest from this warped Finnish one man band, an

avant electronic offshoot of rhythmic black metal minimalists Ride For

Revenge. It's a vinyl-only release on the White Denim label, who

actually discovered WOM via AQ's championing of 'em! This lp is a

hypnotic, head spinning sprawl of blackened kraut-noise synth-drone

power-electronic minimalism, spaced out and droney, slow shifting

layers, constantly morphing textures, pulsing and pulsating, the

sounds raw and gristly, the tracks maniacally repetitive and cyclical,

total tranced out bliss.

DEVO: Heck yeah, the absolutely essential early Devo demos & rarities

collections, Hardcore Vols. 1 & 2, at long last reissued, by our pals

at Superior Viaduct, on vinyl (for the first time we've ever seen),

and as a double cd too. Crucial, catchy, pioneering synth-punk

perversity circa 1974-'77, from one of the best bands ever, at their

most raw and underground. For fans, obviously, AND anyone who still

needs to be exposed to the surreal, subversive genius of Devo. Four

bonus tracks.

QUTTINIRPAAQ: Hot on the heels of a split with psychedelic drone-drug-

doom heavies Bong, comes the vinyl-only debut full length of heavy

psych / caustic noise / kraut-sludge pummel from this Texas outfit,

featuring members of former Record Of The Weekers Rubble as well as

Shit And Shine offshoot outfit Same Sac! A twisted tangle of drug

addled Southern psych, knuckle dragging motorik murk and slo-mo black

noise doom-dirge crumble.

And then there are quite a few Highlights as well, an A-Z of

awesomeness from some old faves as well as new ones, including several

new titles on the cult Khrysanthoney label, also several on a cool new

drone label that exists to support environmental/animal charities

called Oaken Palace, plus plenty more in the way of metal and psych

and hiphop and noiserock and other goodness... We probably could have

picked quite a few more Records Of The Week out of this batch, with

Locrian and Rob Jo Star Band and Cough Cool and Dethscalator all among

the many contenders!

ACID MOTHERS TEMPLE: Another killer new record from these uber-

prolific Japanese heavy psych masters!

ADORAN: New record from this Aidan Baker (Nadja) project, super buzzy

and heavy, instrumental, droned out, roiling slow-mo, low end dirgery.

AGNES: New super limited cassette of gritty blackened textures and

carcinogenic dronemusic from this mysterious soundscapetress.

ALESSANDRO ALESSANDRONI: Classic Italian library music reissued on

vinyl! Gorgeous choral music mixed with Italo-lounge and Bossa

moodiness.

ALTAR OF PLAGUES: Latest blast of epic post black metal from these

Irish heavies, equal parts immersive instrumental drone and frantic

blurring blast.

THOMAS BEL: Super limited cd-r of desolate moody ambience, field

recording flecked murk and beautifully lilting, maudlin, super

stripped down mope-rock dreaminess.

BLACK THREAD: Latest tape from this Von Himmel offshoot, blurred,

washed out, decayed soft noise loveliness for fans of Philip Jeck, the

Caretaker and William Basinski.

BOARDS OF CANADA: Also on cd! Brand new full length from this beloved

mysterious electronic duo, their darkest yet, sinister and soundtracky!

BOTANIST: Now on vinyl! Full length number FOUR from this local eco-

terroristic, drum and dulcimer driven black metal one band band!

Shoegaze-ier than ever.

BRAINBOMBS: Finally available on vinyl! The 2nd singles comp from

these massively misanthropic, scuzzy Swedes!

CAUCHEMAR: Debut full length from this Canadian doomy true metal crew,

fronted by Annick Giroux, of Hellbent For Cooking: The Heavy Metal

Cookbook fame, more trance inducing classic doom / vintage speed metal

jams for fans of Pagan Altar, Witchfinder General, Acid and NWOBHM.

CAUDAL: Super limited new lp from yet another Aidan Baker (Nadja)

project, two sides of gorgeous, laid back, washed out, softly

psychedelic sprawl. On Oaken Palace.

COUGH COOL: Full length number three from this mysterious slo-mo

psychedelic bedroom shoegaze dirge pop duo, a bit more traditionally

indie rock, but still murky and psychedelic and woozily rhythmic.

DARK: Reissue of cult seventies British fuzz prog rarity, featuring

Wicked Lady axeman.

DEAFHEAVEN: Finally on cd, record number two of soaring epic post-rock

tinged black metal from this local outfit.

DEMDIKE STARE: Latest in this mysterious duo's series of more dance

oriented 'test pressings' releases.

DETHSCALATOR: First full length from this UK noiserock crew, dubbed

out (?), pig fuck heaviness, like a revved up Brainbombs or (slightly)

less drug addled Rusted Shut! Super limited bubblegum colored vinyl!

DIGABLE PLANETS: This nineties hip hop classic reissued on vinyl.

EKIN FIL: A jawdroppingly gorgeous piece of slumbering drone-pop from

this mysterious soundscaper/songstress.

EMBUDAGONN 108: Super limited lp of twisted Japanese outsider, audio

collage / avant noise weirdness. Super deluxe packaging.

KAT EPPLE & BOB STOHL: New on Demdike Stare's Dead-Cert label, this

fantastic collection of early space music / new age.

FREE FALL: Debut from super retro rocking Swedish outfit, in the vein

of AC/DC and UFO, ex-The Soundtrack Of Our Lives.

GLOCHIDS: A seriously twisted, and gorgeously noisy collection of

field recording driven, 20th century beholden, fractured avant collage

soundscapery.

STEVE GUNN: First album from psychedelic axe slinger Gunn (GHQ, Magik

Markers), with a full band, some seriously lush, twangy country rock

goodness.

HABSYLL: Warehouse find, last copies of this kick-ass tUMULt release

from a few years back, twisted slo-mo avant sludge dooooooooooom!

HOLY FAMILY: Super limited tape from this Lawrence English led duo,

who fuse the classic psychedelic minimalist drones of groups like the

Taj Mahal Travellers to the more noisy, blown out floor-core of modern

synth/drone combos.

HOT LUNCH: Also on cd! Latest from these badass San Francisco skate-

rockers, includes an ELP cover!!

I AM A LAKE OF BURNING ORCHIDS x 2: Two new super limited cd-r's of

blown out, shoegaze, electro-noise, bliss-pop 'dreamo', one disc

crumbly and abrasive, the other hushed and drifty. On Khrysanthoney.

KING BLOOD: Brand new 7" from our favorite lo-fi, distoto boogie,

zoner-psych one man band!!

KOROUVA: Famously 'stolen' by black metal weirdos Velvet Cacoon, a

vinyl reissue of this infamous slab of brooding, gloom / goth slowcore

balladry, for fans of Chelsea Wolfe and Zola Jesus. On Khrysanthoney.

LLYR: Second tape from these local black metallers, and yet another

twisted mix of grim black buzz and post industrial blackened

electronica. On local tape label Brotherhood Of Light.

LOCRIAN: Latest from these black buzz dronescapers, whose sound has

now blossomed into something more structured and composed, epic and

melodic, and reminding us at times of M83, Jesu or Mogwai!

MAGIC CIRCLE: Now available on vinyl! Debut disc of majestic Sabbathy

doom from these former Boston hardcore luminaries!

THE MANTLES: Latest batch of classic fuzzy sixties style garage pop

jangle from these aQ faves.

MEADS OF ASPHODEL: Yet another weird one from this UK black metal

horde, this one a concept record all about the Holocaust, meant more

MELANKOLI: Gorgeous blast of blissed out shoegazey depressive black

buzz from this Russian one man band. On Khrysanthoney.

MINOT: Head spinning blast of dense progged out math rock radness from

this local outfit.

NADJA: Brand new limited lp of hushed brooding loveliness/heaviness

from this aQ beloved duo, on Oaken Palace.

TOMONARI NOZAKI: Super limited cd of gorgeous dreamlike haze, and

woozy washed out crumble, a hushed slowly decaying sprawl worthy of

the Caretaker or William Basinski.

OOOOO: Latest collection of dark brooding slo-mo electronica from this

hard to pronounce SF witch house warlock.

PARALLEL LINES ///: Killer lp of sprawling blissed out drone-psych

ROB JO STAR BAND: Finally reissued, this ultra obscure, freaky French

'70s underground psychedelic proto punk classic, with bonus tracks!

ROSE WINDOWS: First full length of Eastern tinged slow burn psych,

Sub Pop.

SATAN: First record in THIRTY years from these NWOBHM legends,

original lineup too!

SHAHRAM: Best of from this seventies Iranian psychedelic groove

merchant.

SMITH WESTERNS: Full length number three from these Midwestern jangle

poppers.

SPECTRALS: Latest batch of twang flecked indie pop from this UK

outfit, on Slumberland.

THE SPYRALS: New full length of retro psychedelic garage rock radness

from these local psych rockers.

PAULINE ANNA STROM: Super limited tape reissue of this lost eighties

new wave gem, from right here in SF!

TONY TEARS: New disc of demos and rarities from this Italian

bit proggy.

UNSOUND: Another unearthed original issue of this legendary seminal

industrial culture, performance-art and outsider shock tactics! This

V/A LIBERATION MUSIC: Killer survey of the Flying Dutchman label,

WILL OVER MATTER "Phenomenal Highways" (White Denim) lp 14.98

Back in 2011, we made Might Of The Planet Eater, the debut from

weirdo minimal blackened power electronic one man band Will Over

Matter our Record Of The Week. We initially discovered WOM via the

fact that the 'one man' behind that one man band, was none other than

Harald Mentor, mastermind of aQ beloved Finnish rhythmic black metal

minimalists Ride For Revenge, who for some crazy reason, we just

realized, have never nabbed Record Of The Week honors, cuz in

retrospect, any/all of their previous records were more than deserving.

As we mentioned in our reviews of the other Will Over Matter

records, if you thought Ride For Revenge were weird, then prepare

theyself to be blown away, baffled, confused, frustrated, thrilled,

unnerved, disturbed, most likely all of the above. Will Over Matter

takes the dense rhythmic churn, already sort of spaced out and

abstract, and replaces the bass and drums and traditional instruments,

with what sounds like a battery of cobbled together electronics, home

made machines and invented instruments, it's tough not to envision

Mentor as some sort of mad scientist, in his murky musical laboratory,

surrounded by walls of primitive electronics, old computers, blinking

lights, blown speakers, a room full of towering obelisks of obsolete

technology, harnessed into something otherworldly, and improbably next

level. The sounds these machines emit glitchy and gristly, jagged and

hiss-drenched, in some ways the template for WOM is the same as it was

for Ride For Revenge, the 'band' locked into simple, lurching,

lumbering motorik grooves, the 'beat' wrapped in dense sonic swirls,

thick fields of static, everything glitched out and in-the-red. A head

spinning sprawl of blackened kraut-noise synth-drone power-electronic

minimalism, that RULES. Which brings us to the latest missive from

Mentor, which is another psychedelic minimal electro-noise WTF doozy,

the vocals kept to a minimum this time, the three lengthy tracks,

spaced out and droney, slow shifting layers, constantly morphing

textures, pulsing and pulsating, the sounds raw and gristly, the

tracks maniacally repetitive and cyclical, in a way that to our ears

is total tranced out bliss, but to less adventurous ears, might be

just the opposite. The lack of 'song' and 'structure', not that there

was that much of either on past WoM release, but even less so here, is

precisely what makes Phenomenal Highways a whole different beast, at

times, it's like past WoM records stripped down to their very skeletal

essence, the opening track some sort of primitive kosmische ritual, a

single synth locked and looped into a seemingly endless cycle of

simple pulses, weirdly warbly and super distorted, a heartbeat like

pulse wreathed in filthy, crusty synth squelch, that squirms and

oozes, occasionally shaped into actual melodies, but more often than

not droning and buzzing hypnotically, the final few minutes, finds the

sounds transformed into weird lazer blast like blurts before suddenly

blinking out.

The second track is even MORE minimal, a super spare soundscape

that sounds like some mysteriously manipulated alien field recording,

something you might here on Editions Mego, a field of crumpled low end

crackle, muted and murky, peppered with little bits of percussion, the

sound a weird bit of grinding muddy noise, it's not until 7 minutes in

that the sound splinters, the background smoothing out into a whirring

distorted drone, while Mentor delivers some demonic sermon over the

top, his voice processed and doused in distortion, before the track

explodes into a field of grinding electronics, tangled melodies,

swirling tones, a robotic psych-noise freakout that manages to be more

listenable than it has any right to be, a head spinning psychedelic

churn that blossom into a final super blown out speaker shredding

blowout, before oozing into the final track, a weird shuffling sprawl

of rib cage rattling low end, dense subsonic tones delivered through

what sounds like slow motion helicopter rotor wash, weirdly rhythmic

and mesmeric, this minimal ooze and creep soon joined by strange

curlicues of melody, the deep tone gathering all sorts of acoustic

crunch as it goes, and once again, about seven minutes in, a super

distorted blast of fractured blown out melody surfaces, giving the

whole thing a seriously psychedelic vibe, those tones totally

overtaking the rest of the sound, before fading out, and returning us

to the slow-swell pulsations of the first half, only to fade out into

a crackly sprawl of sculpted feedback, wreathed in hiss and hum, the

sound smoldering and sinister, draped over a buried barely there

rhythm, some echo drenched vocals in there too, everything sort of

washed out and blurry, drifting beneath sheets of feedback and fields

of staticky hum, all culminating in a final freaked out psych-noise

squall, that does little to disguise the haunting melodic beauty that

lurks within.

As always, amazing, bizarre and bafflingly brilliant, a

dizzyingly abstract, twisted industrial psychedelic drone-noise

masterpiece. That is of course also extremely limited...

DEVO "Hardcore" (Superior Viaduct) 2cd 23.00

Gonna try to keep this relatively short. 'Cause it would be easy

to go on and on and on about what an amazing band Devo were back in

the day and how absolutely ESSENTIAL these newly reissued collections

of the earliest recorded Devo music are. Never before released on

vinyl in the States, the original two volumes of Hardcore Devo came

out on cd and cassette on Rykodisc in 1990 and 1991 respectively, and

have been out of print (and sought after by fans) for many years. Now

at last they've been reissued, as separate volumes on vinyl (one a

single lp, the other double) and together as a double cd set.

Those of you already into Devo, who don't already have this,

NEED this - and even if you do have it, you might want it again,

either to get the vinyl versions, and/or for the 4 previously

unreleased bonus tracks now included (on disc two / Volume Two). For

others, well this is our opportunity to proselytize for the cult of

Devo. Some folks out there who aren't that familiar with Devo might

think of 'em as just some novelty new wave nerd-rock band doing goofy

songs and wearing silly hats, one-hit wonders with 1980's "Whip It!".

And while they did wear silly hats and were plenty goofy, they were

also super subversive and bizarre, both sonically and ideologically,

true pioneers in the realm of synth-punk and new wave, coming up with

a sound that somehow grafted Kraftwerk to punk rock before either were

popular, promoting their own anticapitalist, antiestablishment agenda

couched in irony and Dadaism. These early recordings, demos and

rarities recorded on a 4-track in Akron, Ohio circa 1974-1977, reveal

Devo at their most underground, raw and stripped down, but already

possessed of both catchy songwriting brilliance and conceptual

originality. Definitely not the hippie boogie rock popular at the time!

It's worth noting that both future Devo members Gerald Casale

and Mark Mothersbaugh had been students at Kent State, SDS activists

in fact, and Casale personally witnessed the shocking National Guard

shooting of anti-war demonstrators there in 1970. In some ways, Devo

was the direct result. It's just that their idea of "protest music"

was a bit different that the norm up to that point.

These DIY recordings sound pretty darn good - Devo knew what

they were doing, even before they hooked up with producer Brian Eno

for their 1977 debut Q: Are We Not Men? A: We Are Devo! recorded at

Conny Plank's studio in Germany. In fact, these are the recordings

that got Eno interested in working with them in the first place.

On Volume One there's early versions of such Devo classics as

"Jocko Homo", "Mongoloid", and their brilliant cover of "(I Can't Get

No) Satisfaction", but otherwise it's a plenitude of (sub)genius

tracks never re-recorded for any of their studio albums, though some

were released as singles on their own Booji Boy label, or later as B

sides. It's a treasure trove of prime Devo at their purest and most

urgently devolved!!! It contains one of our all time favorite Devo

toons: "Auto Modown", a simple slice of proto-no-wave funk with a

stinging guitar riff and Mark Mothersbaugh crooning ghoulish lyrics

about how "an acid head goon in a '55 Dodge didn't mean to do it / but

a sidewalk run in the noonday sun ten to one he had to lose it".

Similar traits are shared by most of the tracks on here - the

distorted synth-ed out soundz, the trebly bug zapper guitar, the

absurd lyrical content, the robotically rhythmic catchiness. If you're

a spud who hasn't heard "Social Fools", "Buttered Beauties",

"Mechanical Man", "I'm A Potato", or "Ono" (and the list goes on), you

haven't lived. EVERYTHING on here is great, all 15 cuts. Like we said,

we could go on and on, about such treats as the blissed out, moody

"Golden Energy" or the perversely humorous "Midget", who has the body

of a 2 and half year old baby but the brain of a man, and takes

advantage of the situation - deviant, juvenile, and sometimes

frustrated sexual humor being one foundation of Devo's aesthetic for

sure, with an ironic misogyny certainly being a component of the

band's radical and often misunderstood critique of American culture.

Meanwhile disc two / Volume Two is even more packed with tracks,

25 of 'em, including the rather un-PC "Bamboo Bimbo", the anthemic "Be

Stiff", the raunchy "I Need A Chick", and the electronically spazzed

"U Got Me Bugged" (sung by Booji Boy himself), among many other

weirdass classics. Overall Volume Two tends towards the slightly hit-

and-miss in comparison to the totally solid start-to-finish Volume

One, the Volume Two material not always as tightly on target, but

after all there's more here, and even the lesser tracks are still

worth absorbing repeatedly. Again, there's only a few songs that

surfaced again later on their studio albums, like "Clockout" and their

cover of "Working In A Coal Mine", otherwise it's mostly stuff only

dedicated Devo-tees have heard before. And maybe hardly anyone has

ever heard the four bonus tracks now tacked on to the end of Volume

Two, but they're a worthy, weird bunch, including a couple of that

sound like twisted, tweaked blues, in particular the the electronics-

invaded and lyrically crude "Hubert House".

If you couldn't tell, we're so thrilled this is back in print,

what can we say: BUY IT!! It might not change your life, but it could.

It should. Kudos to the Superior Viaduct label, they've been putting

out all kinds of cool reissues lately, but for us, this is the biggest

deal yet, even if these albums aren't the rarest that they've

reissued. They're nicely done, of course, including amusingly enthused

liner notes by Henry Rollins, someone who has quite obviously drunk

deeply of Devo's Kool-Aid. Heed his words: "Spud, prepare to be amazed!"

ADORAN "s/t" (Consouling Sounds) cd 12.98

The last few non-Nadja Aidan Baker releases have found Baker

delving deeper into a hushed minimalism, which was definitely always a

part of his sound. In the old days it seemed like Nadja records were

loud and heavy, and his solo records were hushed and haunting,

recently though, the sound Baker seems to gravitate toward is a sort

of Necks like minimal skitter, still hushed and haunting for sure, but

more rhythmic, and often more jazzy, whether it be the brooding torch

songs of his Already Drowning record, or the psychedelic krautrocky

shimmer of Smudging, a record he made with his brother.

drum and bass duo in which Baker handles the drums, and at first, at

least for a minute or two, it did sound like that's what we were in

for, albeit with a thick swath of buzzing bass over the top, but as

the first 27 minute track unfurls, it reveals itself as something WAY

heavier, the drums a simple driving pound, the bass thick and

distorted, there's definitely an OM vibe going on, not hard to imagine

those distinctive chanted vocals swooping in, but instead, the duo

lock in tight for the long haul, dishing out a totally instrumental,

droned out, roiling, slomo low end dirge, one that's super hypnotic

and super heavy - and it only gets heavier as it goes, with the bass

getting more and more cranked, spitting out thick chordal washes and

huge gouts of buzzing rumble, while Baker pounds away beneath, the

song seesawing between trancey churn and almost shoegazey blown out

thrum.

ALTAR OF PLAGUES "Teethed Glory & Injury" (Profound Lore) cd 13.98

The phrase "post black metal" gets bandied around quite a bit

these days. It's seemingly become a catch-all for any BM-leaning band

that doesn't wear corpse paint and/or spikes and doesn't shriek about

Satan and/or Odin, and when there's so much rampant experimentation

going on in a genre it can create a certain desensitization. Nothing's

shocking anymore! That's why it's all the more rare nowadays for a

band to emerge from the black metal scene that could truly be

considered challenging and progressive, but Ireland's Altar of Plagues

are most certainly one of those bands. Teethed Glory and Injury is,

first and foremost, massive. An enormously modern rebuttal to metal's

often backwards-looking tendencies. The album shifts seamlessly from

immersive instrumental drone to frantic blurring blast beats with a

surgical precision. The decayed buzz of the ambient passages (which

make up a pretty big portion of the record) perfectly compliment the

more "metal" tracks without ever feeling like filler. Instead they

feel just as essential, giving the record a sense of patience and an

ebb/flow not often found in this sort of band. The effect is oddly

hypnotic and peaceful, even if at the same time the album is often

undeniably crushing and unnerving. When it isn't lulling you into a

false sense of security with its more spacious moments, it's

absolutely pummeling you with an overwhelming aural assault of hyper

blasts, chilling frigid vokillz and calculated mesmerizing riffs, like

crackling transmissions relayed from an alternate future where the

decomposing urban desolation of early Godflesh and Swans have replaced

the usual Bathory/Darkthrone blueprint as the primary black metal

template. The result is singular and thrilling - a brief and

terrifying glimpse at the ruined dystopic landscape black metal will

one day become. But first we'll have to wait for all of those other

bands to catch up!

Released Sound. This French musician has quietly hand-fabricated his

share. To Dye Every Night is something of a personal soundtrack for an

emotional landscape of melancholy and introspection. The materials are

spare but wholly elegant - field recordings of a rain storm, guitar,

soundscaping. Limited to just 100 copies and hand assembled by the
