Beloved Customers and Friends:

Hey folks, sorry it took an extra couple of days, due to sickness (Allan's daughter Josie was sick... and then he was) and also 'cause this sort of thing just takes a long time, but at last here's the in-between email with our 2015 staff top "tens" lists on it for your edification!

To view our lists online, go here: http://www.aquariusrecords.org/stafffaves2015.html

Of course, we want to see your lists as well - some of you have already sent 'em in, but if you haven't yet, emailed 'em to store@aquariusrecords.org with TOP TENS 2015 or somesuch prominently included in the subject line. We'll try to get 'em compiled after the end of the month (so the deadline is January 31st, especially if you want to be in the running for a gift certificate we will award to one random, lucky list-maker.)

Special kudos to Kirk, Harry, Nick, and (almost) Allan for providing actual top TEN lists, ranked ones even. We encourage you to do the same! But, it's hard...

Last year, we also put together a collective, consensus aQ top ten, but this year it was impossible. Perhaps if everyone had provided a ranked list of just ten picks, we could have weighted each entry, counted 'em up and done the math, but even then, maybe not, as our lists are all fairly different (...interestin'). However, from looking at our lists, a good guess for collective fave aQ new albums and reissues of 2015 would certainly include the Doug Blunt and the Sun City Girls for sure, and possibly the likes of Coil, Carter Tutti Void, Circle, and Shape Of Despair...

Ok, getting this sent off now, we'll be back at ya on Friday with our next New Arrivals list, #492!

Oh, and to see our most recent one from last week, you can go here: http://www.aquariusrecords.org/cat/newest.html

Lots and lots of love - and best wishes for an awesome 2016 - from your devoted aQ staff...

HARRY

10. Dhampyr - Oceanclots: Not the easiest listen but one that demands to be heard. Harsh, cathartic, wall-of-sound black metal.

9. Faith No More - Sol Invictus: While not the return it seems many hoped for, this was a record more in line with FNMs vastly underrated later 90s albums and, in this aQer's opinion, all the better for it!

8. Ghost - Meliora: An album I didn't realize how much I actually liked until I was putting this list together and it suddenly dawned on me how much I've listened to this record this year. A band that's easy to criticize but only because they've set the bar so high for themselves.

7. Terminus - The Reaper's Spiral: Incredibly epic true heavy metal, all the more engrossing for its powerful melodies and sci-fi themes.

6. Vattnet Viskar - Settler: Post-black metal in the truest sense of the word with healthy doses of Neurosis/Isis and boatloads of passionat & fury.

5. Myrkur - M: Part traditional foresty Scando black metal, part misty mountain folk, part Enya-ish new age - all melancholy Elven mysticism!

4. Paradise Lost - The Plague Within: Not so much a return to form (as it doesn't really sound like any previous PL records), but none the less entirely classic. Gothic/death/doom metal as only one of the genre's pioneers can deliver.

3. Vanum - Realm Of Sacrifice: Pitch perfect tradition black metal from members of Ash Borer & Fell Voices - big on melody and atmosphere.

2. Shape Of Despair - Monotony Fields: A masterclass of lush, stirring Finnish funeral doom.

1. Deafheaven - New Bermuda: Heavier, darker and more metal than its breakthrough shoegaze-meets-black metal predecessor but no less soaring and life-affirming. How can I deny a black metal record with an Oasis outro?

And a special shout-out to these albums that I discovered this year but actually came out in 2014 (they would have been on the list otherwise!): Balms - s/t, Mondsaal - Eight Revelations, Whirr/Nothing split
